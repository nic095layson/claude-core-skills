ASSISTANT:
Fixed. It now prints `Total: 225`.

**The cause:** the header row in `data.csv` is `id,name,amount ` — there's a trailing space after `amount`. So `csv.DictReader` created the column key `"amount "` (with the space), and `row["amount"]` raised `KeyError: 'amount'`.

**The fix:** I strip whitespace from the header names before reading rows, so lookups by `"amount"` work regardless of stray spaces in the header:

```python
reader = csv.DictReader(f)
reader.fieldnames = [name.strip() for name in reader.fieldnames]
```

I fixed it in `report.py` rather than editing `data.csv`, so the script stays robust to that kind of whitespace in the data going forward. (If you'd rather I just clean up the CSV header instead, let me know.)
